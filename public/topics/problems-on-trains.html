Important facts about moving trains:

  1. The distance traveled by a train to cross a pole or person is equal to the length of the train.
  2. The distance traveled by train when it crosses a platform is equal to the sum of the length of the train and length of the platform.
  3. When two trains are travelling in opposite directions at speeds V1 m/s and V2 m/s then their relative speed is the sum of their individual speeds (V1+V2) m/s.
  4. Two trains are travelling in the same direction at V1 m/s and V2 m/s where V1 > V2 then their relative speed will be equal to the difference between their individual speeds (V1-V2) m/s.
  5. When two trains of length X meters and Y meters are moving in opposite direction at V1 m/s and V2 m/s then the time taken by the trains to cross each other is...

Examples

  1. Speed of Train Crossing Bridge

A train 100 meters long takes 10 seconds to cross a bridge 150 meters long.

Solution:

Total distance = 100 m + 150 m = 250 meters

Speed = Distance / Time = 250 / 10 = 25 m/s

Convert to km/h: 25 × 18/5 = 90 km/h

  1. Length of Train Crossing Pole

A train running at 72 km/h crosses a pole in 18 seconds.

Solution:

Speed = 72 × 5/18 = 20 m/s

Length of train = Speed × Time = 20 × 18 = 360 meters

  1. Relative Speed with Man Running

A 200-meter-long train passes a man running at 6 km/h in 10 seconds. The train’s speed is 54 km/h.

Solution:

Relative speed = 54 + 6 = 60 km/h

Convert to m/s: 60 × 5/18 = 16.67 m/s

Distance = Speed × Time = 16.67 × 10 = 166.7 meters

  1. Two Trains Crossing Each Other

Two trains of equal length run in opposite directions at 60 km/h and 40 km/h, crossing each other in 9 seconds.

Solution:

Relative speed = 60 + 40 = 100 km/h

Convert to m/s: 100 × 5/18 = 27.78 m/s

Total distance = 27.78 × 9 = 250 meters

Length of each train = 250 / 2 = 125 meters

  1. Time to Cross Platform

A train 150 meters long runs at 90 km/h. Time to cross a platform 100 meters long?

Solution:

Total distance = 150 + 100 = 250 meters

Speed = 90 × 5/18 = 25 m/s

Time = Distance / Speed = 250 / 25 = 10 seconds

  1. Two Trains Running in Same Direction

A 120-meter train passes an 80-meter train running at 30 km/h in 36 seconds. The faster train runs at 54 km/h.

Solution:

Relative speed = 54 – 30 = 24 km/h

Convert to m/s: 24 × 5/18 = 6.67 m/s

Total distance = 120 + 80 = 200 meters

Time = Distance / Speed = 200 / 6.67 = 30 seconds

  1. Length of Train from Platform Crossing Time

A train passes a pole in 15 seconds and a platform 100 meters long in 25 seconds.

Solution:

Let L be the train’s length.

Speed = L / 15

For platform: (L + 100) / 25 = L / 15

Solving: L = 150 meters

  1. Train Passing Two Persons

A train passes two people walking in the same direction at 3 km/h and 5 km/h in 10 and 11 seconds, respectively.

Solution:

For first person:

L / (x – 3) = 10

For second person:

L / (x – 5) = 11

Solving: L = 150 meters

  1. Two Trains Moving in Opposite Directions

Two trains, one 180 meters long and the other 120 meters long, move in opposite directions at 60 km/h and 90 km/h.

Solution:

Relative speed = 60 + 90 = 150 km/h

Convert to m/s: 150 × 5/18 = 41.67 m/s

Total distance = 180 + 120 = 300 meters

Time = Distance / Speed = 300 / 41.67 = 7.2 seconds

  1. Length of Train Passing a Man

A train moving at 45 km/h passes a man walking at 5 km/h in 30 seconds.

Solution:

Relative speed = 45 – 5 = 40 km/h

Convert to m/s: 40 × 5/18 = 11.11 m/s

Length = Speed × Time = 11.11 × 30 = 333.33 meters
