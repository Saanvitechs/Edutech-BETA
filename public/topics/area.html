Geometric Area Calculations

Basic Concepts

Calculation of the surface area of various geometric shapes.

Key Formulas

Area of a rectangle = Length × Breadth

Area of a triangle = ½ × Base × Height

Area of a circle = π × Radius²

Applications

Problems involving finding the area of land, rooms, etc.

Points to Remember

(A) Triangle:

(i) Area of a triangle = ½ × Base × Height

(ii) If a, b, c are the lengths of the sides of a triangle and s = ½ (a+b+c)

(iii) Area of equilateral triangle = ½ × (side)²

Perimeter of an equilateral triangle = 3 × side

(B) Quadrilaterals:

(i) Area of a rectangle = Length × Breadth

(Diagonal)² = (Length)² + (Breadth)²

Perimeter of a rectangle = 2(Length + Breadth)

(ii) Area of Parallelogram = Base × Height

(iii) Area of a square = (side)² = (diagonal)²

Perimeter of a square = 4 × side

(iv) Area of Rhombus = ½ × (Product of diagonals)

(v) Area of 4 walls of a room = 2 × (Length + Breadth) × Height

(vi) Area of trapezium = ½ × (sum of parallel sides) × perpendicular distance between them

(C) Circles

(i) Circumference of a circle = 2πr

(ii) Area of Circle = πr²

(iii) Arc AB = θ / 360 × 2πr, where ∠AOB= θ and O is the center

(iv) Area of sector AOB = θ / 360 × πr²

Some Quicker Methods:

  1. Carpeting a Floor:

(i) Length of the carpet required = Area of the floor / Width of the carpet

(ii) Amount Required = Rate per meter × Length of carpet

  1. Paving with tiles:

(i) Number of tiles required = Area of the floor / Area of one tile

(ii) The side of the largest possible square tile = HCF of length and breadth of the room

  1. Path around a garden, verandah around a room:

(i) Area of verandah outside room = 2 × (width of verandah) × [length + breadth of room + 2(width of verandah)]

(ii) Area of path within garden = 2 × (width of path) × [length + breadth of garden - 2(width of path)]

  1. Rhombus

(i) Area of a rhombus = ½ × (product of diagonals)

  1. If the length and breadth of a rectangle are increased by x% and y% respectively, the area is increased by (x + y + xy)%

Example Problems

Example 1:
Find the area of a rectangle with length 12 meters and breadth 8 meters.

Solution:

Area of a rectangle = Length × Breadth = 12 × 8 = 96 square meters

Example 2:
Find the area of a triangle with a base of 10 meters and height of 5 meters.

Solution:

Area of a triangle = ½ × Base × Height = ½ × 10 × 5 = 25 square meters

Example 3:
What is the area of a circle with a radius of 7 cm?

Solution:

Area of a circle = π × Radius² = 3.14 × 7² = 153.86 square cm

Example 4:
Find the area of an equilateral triangle with a side length of 6 meters.

Solution:

Area of an equilateral triangle = ¾ × √3 × (side)² = ¾ × √3 × 36 = 15.59 square meters

Example 5:
Find the area of a trapezium where the lengths of the parallel sides are 12 meters and 8 meters, and the height is 5 meters.

Solution:

Area of a trapezium = ½ × (sum of parallel sides) × height = ½ × (12 + 8) × 5 = 50 square meters

Example 6:
Find the area of a square with a diagonal of 10 meters.

Solution:

Area of a square = ½ × (diagonal)² = ½ × 10² = 50 square meters

Example 7:
The radius of a sector of a circle is 6 cm, and the angle subtended by the arc at the center is 60°. Find the area of the sector.

Solution:

Area of a sector = θ / 360 × π × r² = 60 / 360 × 3.14 × 6² = 18.84 square cm

Example 8:
A rhombus has diagonals of 12 meters and 16 meters. Find the area of the rhombus.

Solution:

Area of a rhombus = ½ × (product of diagonals) = ½ × 12 × 16 = 96 square meters

Example 9:
The perimeter of a square is 40 cm. Find its area.

Solution:

Side of the square = Perimeter / 4 = 40 / 4 = 10 cm

Area of the square = side² = 10² = 100 square cm

Example 10:
A room has a length of 6 meters, breadth of 4 meters, and height of 3 meters. Find the area of the four walls.

Solution:

Area of 4 walls = 2 × (Length + Breadth) × Height = 2 × (6 + 4) × 3 = 60 square meters
