Height and Distance in Aptitude

Height and Distance problems in aptitude tests primarily deal with trigonometric concepts. These problems often require the application of basic trigonometric ratios—such as sine, cosine, and tangent—to calculate the height of an object or the distance between two points, given some angles and measurements. These types of questions are frequently seen in competitive exams and require a solid understanding of right-angled triangles and trigonometric relationships.

Key Concepts

  • Angle of Elevation: The angle formed between the horizontal and the line of sight when you are looking up at an object.
  • Angle of Depression: The angle formed between the horizontal and the line of sight when you are looking down at an object.

In both cases, the object and the observer form a right-angled triangle, and trigonometric ratios can be applied to find the required height or distance.

Basic Trigonometric Ratios

  • sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent

Where:

  • Opposite is the side opposite to the angle θ.
  • Adjacent is the side next to the angle θ (but not the hypotenuse).
  • Hypotenuse is the longest side of the triangle, opposite the right angle.

Common Formulas for Height and Distance Problems

  1. To find the height (h) of an object:
    h = d × tan(θ)

    Where:

    • d is the distance from the object.
    • θ is the angle of elevation.
  2. To find the distance (d) from an object:
    d = h / tan(θ)

    Where:

    • h is the height of the object.
    • θ is the angle of elevation or depression.

Practice Problems with Solutions

Problem 1:

The angle of elevation of the top of a building from a point 50 meters away is 30°. Find the height of the building.

Solution:

We are given the distance from the building d = 50 meters and the angle of elevation θ = 30°.

h = d × tan(θ)

h = 50 × tan(30°) = 50 × 1/√3 = 50 × 0.577 = 28.85 meters

Problem 2:

A person observes the top of a tree at an angle of elevation of 45°. If the tree is 20 meters tall, how far is the person from the tree?

Solution:

We are given the height of the tree h = 20 meters and the angle of elevation θ = 45°.

d = h / tan(θ)

d = 20 / tan(45°) = 20 / 1 = 20 meters

Problem 3:

The angle of depression of a boat from the top of a 100-meter cliff is 60°. Find the distance of the boat from the base of the cliff.

Solution:

We are given the height of the cliff h = 100 meters and the angle of depression θ = 60°.

d = h / tan(θ)

d = 100 / tan(60°) = 100 / √3 = 57.74 meters

Problem 4:

A ladder leaning against a wall makes an angle of 60° with the ground. If the ladder is 10 meters long, how far is the foot of the ladder from the wall?

Solution:

We are given the hypotenuse h = 10 meters and the angle θ = 60°.

d = h × cos(θ)

d = 10 × cos(60°) = 10 × 0.5 = 5 meters
