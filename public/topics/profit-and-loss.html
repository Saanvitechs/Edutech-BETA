Profit and Loss

Introduction

Profit and loss are fundamental concepts in business and finance, representing the financial outcomes of transactions, investments, or business operations. Understanding these concepts is crucial for evaluating the performance of a business and making informed financial decisions.

Definitions

  • Profit: Profit is the financial gain obtained when the revenue from sales exceeds the costs associated with producing and selling goods or services. It is an indicator of the success and efficiency of a business.
    • Formula:
      Profit = Selling Price (SP) - Cost Price (CP)
    • Types of Profit:
      • Gross Profit: The profit calculated by subtracting the cost of goods sold (COGS) from total revenue.
        Gross Profit = Revenue - COGS
      • Net Profit: The profit remaining after all expenses (including operating expenses, taxes, interest, etc.) have been deducted from total revenue.
        Net Profit = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses - Taxes
  • Loss: Loss occurs when the costs associated with producing or selling goods or services exceed the revenue generated from those activities. It indicates an unsuccessful operation or transaction.
    • Formula:
      Loss = Cost Price (CP) - Selling Price (SP)

Key Concepts

  1. Cost Price (CP): The original price at which a product is purchased or produced. It includes all costs associated with bringing a product to market.
  2. Selling Price (SP): The price at which a product is sold to customers. It is determined based on various factors, including market demand, competition, and perceived value.
  3. Markup: The amount added to the cost price to determine the selling price. It represents the profit margin.
    • Formula:
      Markup = Selling Price - Cost Price
  4. Discount: A reduction in the selling price, often used to encourage sales or clear inventory.
    • Formula:
      Discount = Selling Price × (Discount Rate / 100)
  5. Percentage Profit or Loss: This is used to express profit or loss as a percentage of the cost price or selling price.
    • Profit Percentage:
      Profit % = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
    • Loss Percentage:
      Loss % = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100

Examples

Problem 1
A shopkeeper buys a shirt for $40 and sells it for $60. What is the profit percentage?

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $40
  • Selling Price (SP) = $60
  • Profit = SP - CP = $60 - $40 = $20
  • Profit Percentage = (Profit / CP) × 100 = (20 / 40) × 100 = 50%

Problem 2
A man sells a bicycle for $120 and incurs a loss of 20%. What was the cost price of the bicycle?

Solution:

  • Selling Price (SP) = $120
  • Loss Percentage = 20%
  • Loss = (Loss Percentage / 100) × CP
  • 20% of CP = CP - SP
  • 0.2 CP = CP - 120
  • 0.2 CP + 120 = CP
  • 0.8 CP = 120
  • CP = 120 / 0.8 = $150

Problem 3
A retailer buys an item for $200 and marks it up by 30%. What is the marked price, and if he sells it at a 10% discount, what will be the selling price?

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $200
  • Marked Price = CP + (30% of CP) = 200 + (0.3 × 200) = 200 + 60 = $260
  • Selling Price after discount = Marked Price - (10% of Marked Price)
  • Selling Price = 260 - (0.1 × 260) = 260 - 26 = $234

Problem 4
A book is bought for $75 and sold for $90. Calculate the profit and profit percentage.

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $75
  • Selling Price (SP) = $90
  • Profit = SP - CP = 90 - 75 = $15
  • Profit Percentage = (Profit / CP) × 100 = (15 / 75) × 100 = 20%

Problem 5
If a trader buys a product for $150 and sells it for $120, what is the loss percentage?

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $150
  • Selling Price (SP) = $120
  • Loss = CP - SP = 150 - 120 = $30
  • Loss Percentage = (Loss / CP) × 100 = (30 / 150) × 100 = 20%

Problem 6
A person sold a laptop for $800, resulting in a profit of 25%. What was the cost price?

Solution:

  • Selling Price (SP) = $800
  • Profit Percentage = 25%
  • Profit = (Profit Percentage / 100) × CP
  • 25% of CP = SP - CP
  • 0.25 CP = 800 - CP
  • 1.25 CP = 800
  • CP = 800 / 1.25 = $640

Problem 7
A car is bought for $15,000 and sold for $18,000. What is the profit in dollars and the profit percentage?

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $15,000
  • Selling Price (SP) = $18,000
  • Profit = SP - CP = 18,000 - 15,000 = $3,000
  • Profit Percentage = (Profit / CP) × 100 = (3,000 / 15,000) × 100 = 20%

Problem 8
An item is bought for $250 and sold for $200. What is the loss in dollars and the loss percentage?

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $250
  • Selling Price (SP) = $200
  • Loss = CP - SP = 250 - 200 = $50
  • Loss Percentage = (Loss / CP) × 100 = (50 / 250) × 100 = 20%

Problem 9
If a product is marked at $500 and sold for $450 after giving a discount of 10%, what is the cost price if the seller wants to make a profit of 20%?

Solution:

  • Marked Price = $500
  • Selling Price (SP) = $450
  • Let Cost Price (CP) = x
  • Profit = SP - CP = 0.2x
  • 450 = CP + 0.2CP
  • 450 = 1.2CP
  • CP = 450 / 1.2 = $375

Problem 10
A shopkeeper sells two articles for $500 each. The first article is sold at a profit of 20%, and the second article is sold at a loss of 20%. What is the overall profit or loss?

Solution:

  • Selling Price of First Article = $500
  • Profit Percentage = 20%
  • Cost Price of First Article = SP / (1 + Profit Percentage/100)
  • CP of First Article = 500 / (1 + 0.2) = 500 / 1.2 = $416.67
  • Selling Price of Second Article = $500
  • Loss Percentage = 20%
  • Cost Price of Second Article = SP / (1 - Loss Percentage/100)
  • CP of Second Article = 500 / (1 - 0.2) = 500 / 0.8 = $625
  • Total CP = CP of First Article + CP of Second Article
  • Total CP = 416.67 + 625 = $1041.67
  • Total SP = $500 + $500 = $1000
  • Overall Loss = Total CP - Total SP = 1041.67 - 1000 = $41.67
