Percentage

Introduction

Percentage is a mathematical concept used to express a number as a fraction of 100. It is commonly used in various fields such as finance, statistics, and everyday life to compare quantities and understand relationships between values. Understanding percentages is essential for solving various problems, especially in competitive exams and aptitude tests.

Definition

  • Percentage: A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a part of 100. The symbol for percentage is "%". For example, 45% means 45 out of 100.

Formula

The basic formula to calculate the percentage is:

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Where:

  • Part: The portion of the whole you are interested in.
  • Whole: The total or the entire amount.

Key Concepts

  1. Conversion Between Fractions, Decimals, and Percentages:
    • To convert a fraction to a percentage, multiply by 100.
    • To convert a decimal to a percentage, multiply by 100.
    • To convert a percentage to a fraction, divide by 100 and simplify if necessary.
  2. Finding the Whole from a Percentage: If you know the percentage and the part, you can find the whole using the formula:
Whole = (Part / Percentage) × 100
  1. Finding the Part from a Percentage: If you know the percentage and the whole, you can find the part using the formula:
Part = (Percentage / 100) × Whole
  1. Percentage Increase and Decrease:
    • Percentage Increase: When a value increases, the percentage increase can be calculated using:
Percentage Increase = ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) × 100
    • Percentage Decrease: When a value decreases, the percentage decrease can be calculated using:
Percentage Decrease = ((Original Value - New Value) / Original Value) × 100

Applications of Percentages:

  1. Financial Calculations: Percentages are used in calculating interest rates, discounts, profit margins, and tax rates.
  2. Statistics: Percentages are used to express data in a more understandable form, such as survey results and population statistics.
  3. Comparative Analysis: Percentages help in comparing different quantities, making it easier to analyze trends and changes.

Common Misconceptions

  • A common misconception is confusing "percent" with "percentage point." For example, an increase from 10% to 12% is a 2 percentage point increase, but it represents a 20% increase from the original 10%.
  • Another misconception is thinking that a percentage can only be greater than zero. A percentage can be negative if it represents a decrease.

Examples

Example 1:
A student scored 45 out of 60 in a test. What is the percentage score?

Solution:

  • Percentage = (Score / Total) × 100
  • Percentage = (45 / 60) × 100 = 75%

Example 2:
A product originally priced at $80 is now sold for $60. What is the percentage discount?

Solution:

  • Discount = Original Price - Selling Price
  • Discount = $80 - $60 = $20
  • Percentage Discount = (Discount / Original Price) × 100
  • Percentage Discount = ($20 / $80) × 100 = 25%

Example 3:
If a population of a town is 25,000 and 10% of the population are children, how many children are there?

Solution:

  • Number of Children = Total Population × (Percentage / 100)
  • Number of Children = 25,000 × (10 / 100) = 2,500

Example 4:
A car's value depreciates from $20,000 to $15,000. What is the percentage decrease in value?

Solution:

  • Decrease = Original Value - New Value
  • Decrease = $20,000 - $15,000 = $5,000
  • Percentage Decrease = (Decrease / Original Value) × 100
  • Percentage Decrease = ($5,000 / $20,000) × 100 = 25%

Example 5:
A teacher has 30 students in a class, and 12 of them passed the exam. What percentage of students passed?

Solution:

  • Percentage of Students Passed = (Number of Passes / Total Students) × 100
  • Percentage of Students Passed = (12 / 30) × 100 = 40%

Example 6:
A store increases the price of a shirt from $50 to $65. What is the percentage increase in price?

Solution:

  • Increase = New Price - Original Price
  • Increase = $65 - $50 = $15
  • Percentage Increase = (Increase / Original Price) × 100
  • Percentage Increase = ($15 / $50) × 100 = 30%

Example 7:
In a survey, 75% of participants prefer online shopping. If there are 200 participants, how many prefer online shopping?

Solution:

  • Number Preferring Online Shopping = Total Participants × (Percentage / 100)
  • Number Preferring Online Shopping = 200 × (75 / 100) = 150

Example 8:
A recipe requires 200 grams of sugar, which is 20% of the total weight of the ingredients. What is the total weight of the ingredients?

Solution:

  • Total Weight = Sugar Amount / (Percentage / 100)
  • Total Weight = 200 / (20 / 100) = 1,000 grams

Example 9:
A laptop is sold for $800 after a 20% discount off the original price. What was the original price?

Solution:

  • Let the original price be X.
  • Discounted Price = X - (20% of X) = 0.8X
  • 0.8X = $800
  • X = $800 / 0.8 = $1,000

Example 10:
If you have 150 points out of 200 in a game, what percentage of the total points did you score?

Solution:

  • Percentage Score = (Points Scored / Total Points) × 100
  • Percentage Score = (150 / 200) × 100 = 75%
