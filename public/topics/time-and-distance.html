Key Concepts in Time and Distance

    Speed:

  •  Speed is defined as the distance traveled per unit of time.
  •  Formula: Speed=Distance/Time.

      • Time:

  • Time is the duration taken to cover a particular distance.
  • Formula: Time=Distance/Speed.

      • Distance:

  • Distance is the total path traveled.
  • Formula: Distance=Speed×Time

    •  Relation Between Speed, Time, and Distance:

    • These three quantities (speed, time, and distance) are related. If you know two, you can easily calculate the third.

      Speed = Distance/Time, if we know the value of Distance and Time we can easily calculate Speed

     Important Units:

    • Speed:

      Meters per second (m/s) or kilometers per hour (km/h)

    • Distance:

      Meters (m) or kilometers (km)

    • Time:

      Seconds (s) or hours (hr)

      Conversions:

    • To convert km/h to m/s, multiply by 5/18.
    • To convert m/s to km/h, multiply by 18/5.

    Examples

    1. A train 150 meters long is running at 60 km/h. How long will it take to pass a pole?

    Solution:

    Speed = 60 km/h = (60 × 1000) / 3600 = 16.67 m/s

    Time = Distance / Speed = 150 / 16.67 ≈ 9 seconds

    Answer: 9 seconds

    1. A car travels 300 km in 5 hours. What is its average speed?

    Solution:

    Average speed = Distance / Time = 300 / 5 = 60 km/h

    Answer: 60 km/h

    1. A train running at 80 km/h crosses a platform 100 meters long in 18 seconds. What is the length of the train?

    Solution:

    Speed in m/s = (80 × 1000) / 3600 = 22.22 m/s

    Total distance = Speed × Time = 22.22 × 18 = 400 meters

    Length of train = Total distance - Platform length = 400 - 100 = 300 meters

    Answer: 300 meters

    1. A person walks at 5 km/h for 30 minutes, then runs at 10 km/h for 1 hour. What is his average speed for the entire trip?

    Solution:

    Distance covered while walking = 5 × (30 / 60) = 2.5 km

    Distance covered while running = 10 × 1 = 10 km

    Total distance = 2.5 + 10 = 12.5 km

    Total time = 0.5 + 1 = 1.5 hours

    Average speed = Total distance / Total time = 12.5 / 1.5 ≈ 8.33 km/h

    Answer: 8.33 km/h

    1. A train of 120 meters long crosses a bridge 180 meters long. If the speed of the train is 72 km/h, how long will it take to cross the bridge?

    Solution:

    Total distance = Length of train + Length of bridge = 120 + 180 = 300 meters

    Speed in m/s = (72 × 1000) / 3600 = 20 m/s

    Time = Distance / Speed = 300 / 20 = 15 seconds

    Answer: 15 seconds

    1. Two trains, 100 meters and 150 meters long, are moving in opposite directions at speeds of 50 km/h and 70 km/h, respectively. How long will they take to cross each other?

    Solution:

    Relative speed = 50 + 70 = 120 km/h = (120 × 1000) / 3600 = 33.33 m/s

    Total distance = Length of first train + Length of second train = 100 + 150 = 250 meters

    Time = Distance / Relative Speed = 250 / 33.33 ≈ 7.5 seconds

    Answer: 7.5 seconds

    1. A person covers a distance of 120 meters in 10 seconds while running. What is his speed in meters per second?

    Solution:

    Speed = Distance / Time = 120 / 10 = 12 m/s

    Answer: 12 m/s

    1. A cyclist starts from a point and travels at 12 km/h for 3 hours, then at 18 km/h for 2 hours. What is the average speed for the entire journey?

    Solution:

    Distance while traveling at 12 km/h = 12 × 3 = 36 km

    Distance while traveling at 18 km/h = 18 × 2 = 36 km

    Total distance = 36 + 36 = 72 km

    Total time = 3 + 2 = 5 hours

    Average speed = Total distance / Total time = 72 / 5 = 14.4 km/h

    Answer: 14.4 km/hr

    1. A train travels at a speed of 50 km/h. How long will it take to cover 120 kilometers?

    Solution:

    Time = Distance / Speed = 120 / 50 = 2.4 hours

    Answer: 2.4hours

    1. A man can row a boat at a speed of 4 km/h in still water. If the stream flows at 2 km/h, what will be his speed while going downstream?

    Solution:

    Speed downstream = Speed of boat + Speed of stream = 4 + 2 = 6 km/h

    Answer: 6 km/hr
