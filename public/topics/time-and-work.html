Time and Work

  • Basic Concepts: Deals with the rate of work done by individuals or groups.
  • Key Formulas:
    • Work = Rate × Time
    • Combined work rates for multiple workers.
  • Applications: Problems involving multiple people working together or against each other, time required to complete a job, etc.

Points to remember:

1) If a man can do a piece of work in n days, work done by him in one day = part of total work or he will finish work in one day.

2) If a man completes work in one day, he will complete the entire work in n days.

3) If A can complete a piece of work in X days and B can complete the same work in Y days, both A and B working together can finish the same work in days.

4) If A is thrice as good as workman as B or A can work three times faster than B, the ratio of work done by A and B for the same duration of time will be = 3:1. And the ratio of time taken by A and B to finish the same amount of work will be = 1:3.

Examples

Question 1:

A can complete a task in 10 days, and B can do the same task in 15 days. If they work together, how many days will they take to complete the task?

Solution:

Work done by A in 1 day = 1/10

Work done by B in 1 day = 1/15

Together, work done in 1 day = 1/10 + 1/15 = (3 + 2) / 30 = 5/30 = 1/6

So, A and B together will take 6 days to complete the task.

Question 2:

A can complete a task in 12 days, while B can complete the same task in 8 days. If A and B work on alternate days, starting with A, in how many days will the task be completed?

Solution:

Work done by A in 1 day = 1/12

Work done by B in 1 day = 1/8

In 2 days, A and B together will complete:

= 1/12 + 1/8

= (2 + 3) / 24

= 5/24 of the task.

Total task = 1.

Let the number of full cycles (2 days each) be n.

So, n × 5/24 of the work is completed in 2n days.

If we solve for n, we get n=4.

In 8 days, A and B together complete 20/24 of the task. A will take the next 2/12 days (or 1/6 of the day) to finish it.

So, the total time taken will be 9.33 days (or 9 days and 4 hours).

Question 3:

A and B together can complete a task in 6 days, but A alone can complete it in 9 days. How long will B take to complete the task alone?

Solution:

Work done by A in 1 day = 1/9

Work done by A and B together in 1 day = 1/6

Work done by B in 1 day = 1/6 - 1/9 = (3 - 2) / 18 = 1/18

So, B alone will take 18 days to complete the task.

Question 4:

A can complete 60% of a task in 9 days. How many more days will A take to complete the rest of the task?

Solution:

60% of the task is completed in 9 days.

So, work done in 1 day = 60% / 9 = 6.67% of the task per day.

Remaining work = 40%.

Days required to complete 40% of the task = 40% / 6.67% per day = 6 days.

So, A will take 6 more days to complete the rest of the task.

Question 5:

A and B together can complete a task in 12 days, while B alone can complete it in 20 days. How long will A take to complete the task alone?

Solution:

Work done by A and B together in 1 day = 1/12

Work done by B in 1 day = 1/20

Work done by A in 1 day = 1/12 - 1/20 = (5 - 3) / 60 = 2/60 = 1/30

So, A alone will take 30 days to complete the task.

Question 6:

If 5 workers can complete a job in 20 days, how many more workers are needed to complete the job in 10 days?

Solution:

Let the total work be W.

Work done by 5 workers in 20 days = W.

Work done by 1 worker in 1 day = W / (5 × 20) = W/100.

If the task needs to be completed in 10 days,

Let the number of workers required be N.

Work done by N workers in 10 days = W,

So, N × W/100 × 10 = W.

N = 10 workers.

Since there are already 5 workers, 5 more workers are needed.

Question 7:

A can complete a task in 15 days, and B can complete it in 10 days. If they work together for 5 days, how much of the task will be left?

Solution:

Work done by A in 1 day = 1/15

Work done by B in 1 day = 1/10

Together, work done in 1 day = 1/15 + 1/10 = (2 + 3) / 30 = 5/30 = 1/6.

Work done by A and B together in 5 days = 5 × 1/6 = 5/6.

Remaining work = 1 - 5/6 = 1/6.

So, 1/6 of the task will be left.

Question 8:

A can complete a task in 8 days, B in 12 days, and C in 16 days. How long will it take for A, B, and C together to complete the task?

Solution:

Work done by A in 1 day = 1/8

Work done by B in 1 day = 1/12

Work done by C in 1 day = 1/16

Together, work done in 1 day = 1/8 + 1/12 + 1/16

= (6 + 4 + 3) / 48

= 13/48.

So, A, B, and C together will take 48/13 days ≈ 3.69 days to complete the task.

Question 9:

A and B can complete a task in 10 days, and B and C can complete it in 12 days. If A, B, and C together can complete the task in 8 days, how long will A and C take to complete the task together?

Solution:

Work done by A, B, and C together in 1 day = 1/8

Work done by A and B together in 1 day = 1/10

Work done by B and C together in 1 day = 1/12

Let the work done by A, B, and C in 1 day be A, B, and C.

A + B + C = 1/8

A + B = 1/10

B + C = 1/12.

Subtract (A + B + C) - (A + B) = C = 1/8 - 1/10 = (5 - 4) / 40 = 1/40.

Similarly, find the work for A. A + C = 1 - 1/40 = 9/40.

Question 10:

A is 50% more efficient than B. If A alone can complete a task in 18 days, how long will B take to complete the same task alone?

Solution:

Efficiency of A : Efficiency of B = 150 : 100 = 3 : 2.

So, if A takes 18 days to complete the task,

B will take 18 × (3/2) = 27 days.

So, B alone will take 27 days to complete the task.
