Simple Interest

Definition:

Simple interest is a financial term that refers to the interest calculated on the principal amount, which is the initial sum of money borrowed or invested. It is not compounded, meaning that interest is calculated only on the original principal throughout the investment or loan period.

Formula:

The formula for calculating simple interest is given by:

SI = P × R × T / 100

Where:

  • SI represents the simple interest.
  • P denotes the principal amount (the initial amount of money).
  • R signifies the rate of interest per year, expressed as a percentage.
  • T indicates the time period for which the money is borrowed or invested, measured in years.

Characteristics:

  1. Linear Growth: The amount of interest earned or paid grows linearly over time, directly proportional to the time period and the rate of interest.
  2. Constant Rate: The interest rate remains constant for the entire duration of the investment or loan.
  3. No Compounding Effect: Unlike compound interest, simple interest does not factor in interest on previously earned interest, making calculations straightforward.
  4. Ease of Calculation: Simple interest calculations are generally easier and quicker compared to compound interest calculations, making them accessible for various financial assessments.

Total Amount Calculation:

The total amount accrued or payable at the end of the investment or loan period can be determined by adding the calculated simple interest to the principal amount. This can be expressed as:

A = P + SI

Where:

  • A represents the total amount.
  • P is the principal amount.
  • SI is the simple interest.

Applications:

  • Loans: It is often applied to personal loans, car loans, and some short-term borrowing arrangements.
  • Savings Accounts: Certain savings accounts and fixed deposits might utilize simple interest for short-term investments.
  • Financial Planning: Simple interest is useful in financial planning, budgeting, and forecasting future returns on investments.

Examples:

Example 1

A principal amount of $2,500 is invested at an interest rate of 4% per annum for 5 years. Calculate the simple interest.

Solution:
SI = (2500 × 4 × 5) / 100 = 500

Example 2

If $3,000 is borrowed at a simple interest rate of 6% for 2 years, find the total amount to be repaid.

Solution:
SI = (3000 × 6 × 2) / 100 = 360
Total Amount = Principal + SI = 3000 + 360 = 3360

Example 3

A person invests $10,000 at an annual interest rate of 5% for 3 years. Determine the simple interest earned.

Solution:
SI = (10000 × 5 × 3) / 100 = 1500

Example 4

A loan of $1,200 is taken out at an interest rate of 8% for 4 years. Calculate the interest to be paid.

Solution:
SI = (1200 × 8 × 4) / 100 = 384

Example 5

If you invest $5,500 at a simple interest rate of 7% for 6 years, what will be the total interest accrued?

Solution:
SI = (5500 × 7 × 6) / 100 = 2310

Example 6

A student takes a loan of $800 at an interest rate of 5% for 2 years. Find the simple interest.

Solution:
SI = (800 × 5 × 2) / 100 = 80

Example 7

If a sum of $4,000 is invested for 5 years at a simple interest rate of 10% per annum, what is the simple interest earned?

Solution:
SI = (4000 × 10 × 5) / 100 = 2000

Example 8

A person borrows $600 at a simple interest rate of 9% for 3 years. Calculate the total interest paid.

Solution:
SI = (600 × 9 × 3) / 100 = 162

Example 9

A principal of $1,500 is invested at a rate of 12% per annum for 4 years. Find the simple interest.

Solution:
SI = (1500 × 12 × 4) / 100 = 720

Example 10

If you lend $750 at an interest rate of 11% for 2 years, what will be the total amount received at the end?

Solution:
SI = (750 × 11 × 2) / 100 = 165
Total Amount = Principal + SI = 750 + 165 = 915
