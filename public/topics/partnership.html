Partnership

Key Concepts

  1. Investment Proportion: Profits or losses are distributed according to the ratio of the investments made by the partners. The larger the investment, the greater the share of the profit.
  2. Time Factor: If partners invest their money for different periods of time, the profit share depends not only on how much they invested but also for how long the money was invested. A partner who invests for a longer period gets a higher share.
  3. Profit Sharing Formula:

Share of profit = (Individual’s Investment × Time / Total Investment × Total Time) × Total Profit

Types of Partnership

  1. Simple Partnership: In a simple partnership, all partners invest their money for the same amount of time. The profit (or loss) is shared in the ratio of their respective investments.
    • Example: A and B invest $5000 and $3000 respectively in a business. If the total profit is $8000, what is each partner's share of the profit?

Solution:

Investment ratio of A to B = 5000 : 3000 = 5 : 3

A’s share of profit = (5/8)×8000=5000

B’s share of profit = (3/8)×8000=3000

Thus, A gets $5000 and B gets $3000 from the profit.

  1. Compound Partnership: In a compound partnership, partners invest their money for different periods of time. The profit is divided based on the product of the investment and the time for which the investment was made.
    • Example: A and B invest $4000 and $6000 respectively in a business. A invests for 8 months, and B invests for 6 months. If the total profit is $10,000, how is the profit divided?

Solution:

A’s investment = 4000 × 8 = 32000

B’s investment = 6000 × 6 = 36000

Investment ratio of A to B = 32000 : 36000 = 8 : 9

A’s share of profit = (8/17)×10000=4705.88

B’s share of profit = (9/17)×10000=5294.12

Thus, A gets $4705.88 and B gets $5294.12 from the profit.

Examples

Problem 1
A and B start a business by investing $20,000 and $30,000, respectively. If they earn a total profit of $25,000 at the end of the year, what will be their individual shares of the profit?

Solution:

Investment ratio of A to B = 20,000 : 30,000 = 2 : 3

A's share = (2/5) × 25,000 = 10,000

B's share = (3/5) × 25,000 = 15,000

Problem 2
A, B, and C start a business with investments of $5000, $6000, and $9000, respectively. The total profit is $40,000. How much profit does each partner receive?

Solution:

Investment ratio = 5000 : 6000 : 9000 = 5 : 6 : 9

A’s share = (5/20) × 40,000 = 10,000

B’s share = (6/20) × 40,000 = 12,000

C’s share = (9/20) × 40,000 = 18,000

Problem 3
A and B invest $8000 and $12,000 in a business, respectively. After 6 months, B withdraws half his investment. If the total profit at the end of the year is $20,000, find each partner's share of the profit.

Solution:

A’s investment for the entire year = 8000 × 12 = 96,000

B’s investment = (12,000 × 6) + (6000 × 6) = 72,000 + 36,000 = 108,000

Investment ratio of A to B = 96,000 : 108,000 = 8 : 9

A’s share = (8/17) × 20,000 = 9411.76

B’s share = (9/17) × 20,000 = 10,588.24

Problem 4
A, B, and C invest $12,000, $18,000, and $24,000 respectively in a business. At the end of the year, they earn a profit of $54,000. If B and C receive a total of $45,000 from the profit, how much does A receive?

Solution:

Investment ratio of A : B : C = 12,000 : 18,000 : 24,000 = 2 : 3 : 4

B’s and C’s shares = (7/9) × 54,000 = 42,000

Remaining profit = 54,000 - 42,000 = 12,000

So, A receives $12,000.

Problem 5
X and Y started a business by investing $20,000 and $30,000 respectively. After 4 months, X withdrew $5000, and after another 4 months, Y added $5000. If the total profit is $18,000, what is each partner's share?

Solution:

X’s investment for the first 4 months = 20,000 × 4 = 80,000

For the next 8 months = 15,000 × 8 = 120,000

Total = 80,000 + 120,000 = 200,000

Y’s investment for the first 8 months = 30,000 × 8 = 240,000

For the last 4 months = 35,000 × 4 = 140,000

Total = 240,000 + 140,000 = 380,000

Investment ratio of X to Y = 200,000 : 380,000 = 10 : 19

X’s share = (10/29) × 18,000 = 6,206.90

Y’s share = (19/29) × 18,000 = 11,793.10

Problem 6
A, B, and C enter into a partnership. A invests $2500 for the entire year, B invests $3000 for 8 months, and C invests $4500 for 6 months. If they earn a total profit of $4000, find each partner's share of the profit.

Solution:

A’s investment = 2500 × 12 = 30,000

B’s investment = 3000 × 8 = 24,000

C’s investment = 4500 × 6 = 27,000

Investment ratio = 30,000 : 24,000 : 27,000 = 10 : 8 : 9

A’s share = (10/27) × 4000 = 1,481.48

B’s share = (8/27) × 4000 = 1,185.19

C’s share = (9/27) × 4000 = 1,333.33

Problem 7
A and B invest in a business in the ratio 5:7. After 6 months, C joins the business with an investment of $12,000. If the total profit at the end of the year is $26,400, what is C’s share of the profit?

Solution:

Let A's investment be 5x and B's investment be 7x.

A’s share for 12 months = 5x × 12 = 60x

B’s share for 12 months = 7x × 12 = 84x

C’s share for 6 months = 12,000 × 6 = 72,000

Ratio of A : B : C = 60x : 84x : 72,000

Assume x = 1000. The ratio becomes:

60,000 : 84,000 : 72,000 = 5 : 7 : 6

C’s share = (6/18) × 26,400 = 8,800

Problem 8
A, B, and C start a business with investments of $6000, $8000, and $10,000 respectively. After 6 months, C withdraws half his investment. If the total profit at the end of the year is $14,000, find C's share of the profit.

Solution:

C’s investment for the first 6 months = 10,000 × 6 = 60,000

C’s investment for the next 6 months = 5000 × 6 = 30,000

Total investment = 60,000 + 30,000 = 90,000

Investment ratio of A : B : C = 6 : 8 : 7

C’s share = (7/21) × 14,000 = 4,666.67

Problem 9
A and B enter into a partnership, investing $12,000 and $18,000 respectively. After 4 months, C joins with $24,000. If the total profit after a year is $36,000, find B’s share of the profit.

Solution:

A’s investment = 12,000 × 12 = 144,000

B’s investment = 18,000 × 12 = 216,000

C’s investment = 24,000 × 8 = 192,000

Investment ratio = 144,000 : 216,000 : 192,000 = 6 : 9 : 8

B’s share = (9/23) × 36,000 = 14,086.96

Problem 10
A invests $2500 in a business. After 3 months, B joins with $3500, and after another 6 months, C joins with $4500. At the end of the year, the total profit is $5800. What is B's share of the profit?

Solution:

A’s investment for 12 months = 2500 × 12 = 30,000

B’s investment for 9 months = 3500 × 9 = 31,500

C’s investment for 3 months = 4500 × 3 = 13,500

Investment ratio = 30,000 : 31,500 : 13,500 = 20 : 21 : 9

B’s share = (21/50) × 5800 = 2,436
